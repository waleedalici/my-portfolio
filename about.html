The Evolution of a Clinician

From the Bench to the Population Scale.

My journey began in the precision-driven world of R&D Pharmacy, where molecules were the unit of change. Today, I translate that clinical rigor into the language of Biostatistics and Public Health, bridging the gap between individual patient care and global health outcomes.

MPH Candidate

Specializing in Epidemiology & Biostatistics

The Foundation: R&D Pharmacy

Working at the molecular level taught me the sanctity of protocol. In Pharmacy R&D, there is no margin for error. This period instilled a deep understanding of pharmacokinetics and the biological mechanisms that define clinical success—knowledge that now serves as the biological "ground truth" for my statistical models.

The Pivot: Public Health

The transition to a Master of Public Health was driven by a single question: *How do we scale these individual successes?* I realized that while a pill can save a life, biostatistics can save a community. My background allows me to look at epidemiological data not just as numbers, but as the clinical reality of thousands of patients.
